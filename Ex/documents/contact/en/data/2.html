About DermNet

DermNet is the website of the New Zealand Dermatological Society Incorporated. We aim to present authoritative facts about the skin for consumers and health professionals. The site was launched in March 1996.

Editorial policy

Aims of DermNet NZ

  • To provide authoritative information about the skin
    • All content reviewed by editorial committee
    • All pages prepared by the DermNet Editorial team (unless otherwise specified).
    • Dates of authorship and updates recorded
    • Information about skin diseases has been written for the interested patient rather than the expert and is thus not fully referenced.
  • To be readily accessible and easy to navigate
    • Format read by majority of currently used browsers
    • Low resolution graphics to enhance download
    • Uniform formatting throughout site
    • Search engine can search free text on entire site (except in password-protected areas)
  • To comply with the recommendations of:
    • Criteria for Assessing the Quality of Health Information on the Internet, 1997
    • Health on the Net's Code of Conduct.
    • eHealth Code of Ethics

For in-depth information about individual skin diseases, refer to:

  • emedicine on-line textbook of dermatology, or one of the major conventional published references such as:
    • Rook's Textbook of Dermatology (7th Edition 2004)
    • Fitzpatrick's Dermatology in General Medicine (6th Edition 2003)
    • Bolognia / Jorizzo / Rapini Dermatology 2003

Privacy policy

Confidentiality of data relating to individual patients and visitors, including their identity, is respected by DermNet. The DermNet Development Group undertakes to honour or exceed the legal requirements of medical/health information privacy that apply in New Zealand.

Funding

DermNet NZ is funded by advertising, non-directed sponsorship, directory listings, image sales and Amazon Associate product sales.

Advertising policy

DermNet NZ accepts advertising providing it is relevant to dermatology, ethical, and the DermNet Development Group has approved its format and content. Non-directed sponsorship is also welcomed. Sponsors do not influence content.

Minimum investment is $5,000 per annum. Contact our Secretary & Webmaster Dr Amanda Oakley for further information.
